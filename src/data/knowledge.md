## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Where can I get your resume?**

**Q: How can I contact you?**

**Q: What kind of work do you do?**
A: I'm a Staff Product Designer specializing in AI product design, design systems, and enterprise SaaS. I also focus on bridging gaps between teams, breaking down silos and ensuring everyone feels ownership of their work. Most recently I led the design of Dialpad's first agentic AI platform. **Q: Are you open to new opportunities?**
A: I'm currently happily employed full-time at Dialpad and not actively looking. That said, I'm always open to interesting conversations — feel free to reach out via the Contact form or email.

**Q: What's your design process like?**
A: I start by understanding the people using the product — their goals, their context, the constraints they're working under. From there I move between divergent exploration and convergent decision-making, I lean hard into collaboration with my peers across functional areas, always with a bias toward getting something tangible in front of stakeholders early. I care about craft at every stage: not just visuals, but the clarity of the thinking behind them.

**Q: How do you approach design leadership?**
A: I believe good design culture is as important as good design output. It is very important to me that everyone I work with feels safe and comfortable, because without that, truly good work cannot happen. I will go out of my way to make sure that everyone in the room has a chance to speak if they want to. I try to create the conditions where everyone around me can do their best work — clear direction, honest feedback, and space for people to take ownership. I stay hands-on because I think you lose something important when you stop making things.

**Q: What's your background with AI products?**
A: Starting back in 2022 I was tasked with being the designer in a project at Dialpad called "12 months of AI" in which every month we released an AI driven feature. I learned a lot in this space. After that I was tasked with the design of Dialpad's first agentic AI platform, which meant working through novel interaction patterns, trust and safety considerations, and the challenge of making autonomous systems feel transparent and controllable. I think the most important design work in AI right now is building systems people can actually trust.

## Books

Marshall reads broadly but gravitates toward fantasy and speculative fiction — the kind of books that build entire worlds with internal logic and consequence.

*Authors* J.R.R. Tolkien, Jeff VanDermeer, Frank Herbert, Kurt Vonnegut, Voltaire, Brandon Sanderson, E.E. Cummings

- *The Lord of the Rings* by J.R.R. Tolkien — the gold standard of world-building; formative. Guided so much me becoming who I am
- *Annihilation* (and the Southern Reach trilogy) by Jeff VanderMeer — unsettling, strange, and beautiful. Anything VanderMeer writes is worth reading.
- *Dune* by Frank Herbert — sweeping political sci-fi that rewards re-reads.
- *The Stormlight Archive* by Brandon Sanderson — massive, intricate epic fantasy. If you like fantasy that respects your intelligence, start here.
- *The Once and Future King* by T.H. White - A satirical and political look at Arthurian legend
- *The Guns of August* by Barbara W. Tuchman - An incredibly well written and engaging record of the beginning of WWI

If someone asks what to read, Marshall's go-to recommendations are anything by Jeff VanderMeer if they want something weird and literary, and *The Way of Kings* (first Stormlight book) if they want epic fantasy done right.
